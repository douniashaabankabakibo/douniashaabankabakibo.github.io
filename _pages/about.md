I've always been deeply curious. With a knack for detail and a relentless habit of not letting go of a problem until it is solved, physics felt like a natural fit for me to understand the world at its most fundamental level.

I grew up travelling from one place to another before landing in Montreal with my family in 2014. I learned French quickly enough to pass Université de Montréal's entrance exam within a year, which got me into an undergraduate degree in Mathematics and Physics.

My path through research has been anything but linear — from an internship in particle physics to a master's in mathematical physics to a PhD in computational condensed matter physics. In 2024, I had the chance to intern in Yoshua Bengio's lab at Mila, which cracked open the world of Artificial intelligence (AI) for me. I see AI as a panoply of tools that the curious scientist in me is eager to collect and add to their set. That experience reshaped my doctoral work, and I'm now a fifth-year PhD student at Université de Montréal/Mila, co-supervised by Michel Côté and Alex Hernandez-Garcia, using AI to explore everything from Raman spectra to battery materials and critical phenomena.

Good science communication is central to how I think about my role as a researcher, especially in an era of widespread misinformation. Recent highlights include first place (and the audience prize) at Mila's inaugural science communication competition in 2024 ([Mila Science Communication Contest](https://mila.quebec/en/news/congratulations-to-the-winners-and-finalists-of-milas-first-science-communication-contest)), a feature in UdeMNouvelles ([UdeMNouvelles Article](https://nouvelles.umontreal.ca/article/2025/01/13/dounia-shaaban-kabakibo-la-recherche-fondamentale-au-service-du-tres-concret)), and an appearance on Radio-Canada's Moteur de recherche ([Radio-Canada Episode](https://ici.radio-canada.ca/ohdio/premiere/emissions/moteur-de-recherche/episodes/952105/rattrapage-du-2-avr-2025-les-neuf-limites-ecologiques-et-brasse-camarade-dans-la-ruche)).

Beyond the lab, I'm an elected student representative at Mila, whose role is to empower, represent, and support students. I'm also a co-organizer of the 2024 and 2025 editions of the RQÉMP summer school.

Outside academia, I'm a lifelong arts lover, from needlework, creative writing to music.

I speak English, French, Arabic (y un poco de Espagnol, wanna share a Duolingo friend quest?). 

Feel free to reach out through dounia@umontreal.ca!
